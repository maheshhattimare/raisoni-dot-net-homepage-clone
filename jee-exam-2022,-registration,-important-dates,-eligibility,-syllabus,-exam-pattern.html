The NTA has made several changes for this year's JEE Mains. Given below are the list of new changes levied by the NTA-

Negative-Marking Scheme:

For this year, the NTA has also introduced negative marking for section B. Before this, there was no negative marking for the numerical questions.

Increase in the number of exam centres:

This year, the number of exam centres for JEE Main 2023 exam has been increased to 501 cities in India itself, and for the centres abroad, the count has reached 13 centres. The eligible candidates are allowed to choose four exam centres as per their preference as they fill out their application form for JEE Mains 2023.

Number of attempts:

For the year 2023, the NTA will be conducting the JEE Mains 2023 exams in two separate sessions, one in April 2023 and the second in May 2023.

No correction window:

This year, there will be no correction facility provided by the NTA once the candidate submits the application form.

Eligibility
  • The aspirants should have passed class XII or the required qualifying exam in 2021 to appear for JEE 2023.
  • Aspirants who are appearing for class XII or equivalent qualifying exams in 2023 can also apply.
  • There's no such age restriction to appear for JEE Main 2023 exam. Any candidate who has passed the class XII or its equivalent exam in the year 2021 or is appearing in 2023 can apply for the entrance exam.
  • The aspirants are eligible to appear for JEE Mains for three consecutive years. The entrance test for the same will be conducted four times a year.
  • For B.Tech, he/she should have passed the qualifying exam with Physics, Mathematics, and any one of the following subjects -
    • Chemistry
    • Biology
    • Biotechnology
    • Technical Vocal Subject

STEPS FOR APPLYING JEE MAINS:

The eligible candidates are required to follow the step by step procedure given below.

Step 1 – Registration

The first step consists of filling up the form by providing the basic details about the candidate, such as email address, phone number, and personal details. The Candidates will be required to create a password and answer the security questions for the security of their login id. As they complete their registration, a unique code for their JEE application number will be generated by the system. The candidates are required to save the given registration number as required for all future login requirements.

Step 2 - Completion of the application form

In this step, the candidates will be required to log in to their accounts using the given generated application number along with their password as they submit all the required details for JEE 2023 application form.

Step 3 - Uploading the documents

All the relevant documents need to be uploaded in the specified format.

Specifications for the Documents:

  • Scanned Photograph of the Candidate
    • Size - 10 kb to 200 kb
    • Format - JPG/JPEG
  • Scanned Signature of the Candidate
    • Size - 4 kb to 30 kb
    • Format - JPG/JPEG
  • Class XII Admit card/Marksheet/Certificate of the Candidate
    • Size - 50kb to 500kb
